Itinerary

Here's a basic itinerary that I've followed during winter school ice climbing trips. It is in no way meant to be a mandatory timing but a suggestion for how to run things. Feel free to shift the times and add / remove things as fit for the participants and size of your group.
  • 8:00 am wake up at cabin
  • 9:00 am out of cabin door
  • 9:15-9:45 eat breakfast
  • 10:00-10:30 am at the trailhead
    • go around the circle with names
    • distribute group gear (ropes, stove, first aid kit, axes)
    • discuss itinerary
  • 11:00 am at the flow
    • leader 1 sets up top-rope (try to get more than one line out of each rope)
    • leader 2 discusses "safety and technique"
  • 11:30 participants start climbing, encourage all participants to try all lines (and possible one more than once)
  • 12:30 boil apple cider for participants (cocoa? tea?)
  • 3:00 boil last drinks of the day
  • 3:30 leader 1 does demo on v-threads while leader 2 cleans anchors
  • 4:00 demo on rope coiling / pack up and leave
  • 5:00 back at cars, thank you / good bye (mention gear maintenance like drying off crampons)

Discussions

    Safety
  • always wear helmets / eye protection
  • don't step on the rope (or anything) with your crampons
  • always stand out of fall zone
  • yell ice for big chunks
  • helmet down when ice falls in direction of your face
    Technique
  • hooking
  • swinging the axe with a downward motion, flick of the wrist
  • don't have axe points in a horizontal line
  • kicking with a downward motion, heels down
  • hips and body in close to the wall
    v-threads:
  • what is a vthread?
  • when/ why / where is it used?
  • how do you do it? (then try it out if time permits)
