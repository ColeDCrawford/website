ROCK CLIMBING

MITOC holds numerous climbing trips throughout the year. They include:

  • Circus - Held monthly April through October.
  • Acadia - Held 1-2 times a year in summer months.
  • IOCA - Bi-annual trips with the Intercollegiate Outing Club.

MITOC also holds numerous climbing events, clinics, and courses. Such as:

  • Learn to Climb Series - From top roping to multi-pitch, a great opportunity for beginner climbers.
  • Self Rescue - Intro course for dealing with emergencies in the vertical terrain
  • Technical Review Sessions - week-to-week review sessions to practice skills necessary for climbing

If you would like to go on one of the club trips or be informed of smaller trips led by individual members take place every weekend you should join the MITOC mailing list.

Leadership

Climbing is inherently dangerous, it is better to obtain instruction and advice from a more experienced climber rather than "figuring it out the hard way". As such, MITOC maintains a list of climbing leaders who have demonstrated an interest in both helping out the community as well as attention to safety. As the outing club sponsors sessions throughout the year to learn about climbing skills, this is the group of people that make it possible.
