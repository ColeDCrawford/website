THE RUGGED RAMPAGE 2012

An awesome, 6-hour adventure race brought to you by the MIT Outing Club and MIT’s LEF Fund.

Overview

The race will be held in and around the Charles River and MIT’s campus on Sunday, September 22, 2013 from 9 AM to 3 PM. Teams of 2 or 3 will run, row, climb and bike their way to as many checkpoints as possible during the 6 hour race. Each team will choose their own route through the course navigating with a map and compass (no GPS!) while optimizing their potential to earn points. Teams will earn additional points by solving challenging puzzles along the way. We’ll use an open-ended rogaine-style scoring format to give teams maximal flexibility in developing their strategy. We’ll cap off the day with an awesome BBQ and awards ceremony. Get excited!

Philosophy

Adventure racing is an awesome team sport that involves a combination of physical stamina, technical skill, logistical management, analytical skills and team-work for success. Adventure racing often takes racers to distant wilderness, which can create a steep barrier to entry for newcomers so we wanted to bring that adventure to you in a manageable 6-hour sprint format! The Rugged Rampage is intended as a fun introduction to adventure racing for folks with all sorts of different skills/interests.

Scoring

Checkpoints will be scattered across ~20 miles of trails and buildings around MIT and the Charles River. Checkpoints are marked by standard orange and white controls with a distinctive whole-punch used to mark the scoring passport each team will race with. You will punch your passport to prove you have successfully navigated to each checkpoint. Each checkpoint will be given a different point value dependent on its difficulty. Racers can determine which checkpoints to visit and the order of their traverse. The course is designed to have more checkpoints than any team will be able to visit (even these guys).This will make the course challenging for both experienced and novice teams alike.

Unique Features

As this is an MIT race, we will include various puzzles along the course which teams can solve in order to earn additional points. None of these puzzles will be required, so if you get stumped you can move on, but they’ll be a lot of fun and help you earn extra points. Further details about the nature of the puzzles will be provided at the race start, although the suggested gear list may provide instructive clues.

Can’t race this year? Sign up as a volunteer!

We’ll have free t-shirts, breakfast, lunch and dinner for all of our volunteers. And trust us, you’ll have a great time!

Registration

Pricing:

$20 race fee for non-MIT afficiates ($10 for MIT student/faculty/staff). This helps pay for a nice wicking t-shirt and a post-race BBQ. MIT’s LEF fund is very generously subsidizing the rest of the costs for this event.

$15 membership fee for non-MITOC members (includes 1 year membership to MITOC)

Registration - In order to reserve your spot you must complete the registration form and pay the race fee.

Racer Limits - We will cap the race at 100 participants and post when we've sold out. We will also maintain a waiting list if you miss registration in case others drop out. Register early to be sure you have a spot!

Age Restrictions: All racers must be at least 18 years of age on the day of the race.

Fee: To pay the registration fee, go to this link, and under the Gear Rentals, Trips and Courses heading, select Payment For: Trips. From the menu that appears, select the appropriate Adventure Race option. Leave the Cabin and Boat Rental sections blank.

Race Division

Teamwork and group decesion making are at the core of adventure racing. Racers must register as pairs or triples (no solo racers!). If you're looking for a partner and your friends are lame, try posting to out team-builder forum. Whether its an old buddy or a stranger, be sure to practice orienteering, talk strategy, and ideally train together before race day!
