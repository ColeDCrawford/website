BECOME A BOD

Board of directors (or BOD for short) are the leadership behind MITOC. Our meeting are held monthly and are open to the MITOC community, please check the calendar for future meetings or contact our president.

Being a MITOC officer is fun and interesting. First of all, you meet a great number of interesting people, and gain a better understanding of how the club works. Especially new members benefit from this arrangement, since you will have a chance to go on many more trips, as well as organize some of your own. It will give you a great opportunity to work on your own and to shape the club the way you want it to. The club usually follows the policies of the most active members, and most of the club expansion effort is directed into the areas of greatest activity.

Interestingly, the club is one of the few financially self-sufficient student organizations on campus, earning its operating budget through gear rentals and book sales. As an officer of MITOC, you can bring about changes that will directly benefit a large part of the MIT community. You can take on responsibility for large events, such as Winter School lecture series, or the Circuses.

These experiences are certainly more valuable than just resume stuffers.

BOD Position Descriptions

Below is a more detailed list of responsibilities for each position.

Voting Positions:

President
  • Plan, schedule (doodle), organize (purchase food), and moderate meetings.
  • Fulfill VP/secretary duties when VP/secretary is not fulfilling them.
VP/secretary
  • Take notes at meetings and email notes to mitoc-bod within 1 week after the meeting.
  • Respond to mitoc-owner inquiries and manage mailing lists.
  • Fulfill president duties when president is not fulfilling them.
Bursar
  • Deposit checks collected in bottom safe to SAO, cross check with database records, and document into MITOC treasury records.
  • Prepare annual budget report
  • Fulfill all treasurer duties when treasurer is not fulfilling them
Treasurer
    - Process reimbursements and document into MITOC treasury records.
  • Ensure shift desk workers get paid and document into MITOC treasury records.
  • Check MITOC mail (PO Box and SAO) and pay bill and document into MITOC treasury records.
  • Fulfill all bursar duties when bursar is not fulfilling them.
Board Member x 5
  • Proactive member who assists fellow officers when needed and spearheads bigger (less defined) projects.
  • Fulfill above roles of officers when such officer is not fulfilling them.

Event Chairs:

Winter School Chair
  • Allocates or fulfills the following tasks: WFA/WAFA course, WS retreat, WS lectures, WS guest lectures.
  • Responds to inquiries related to WS.
  • Sets up WS leader application and info session
Circus Chair
  • Schedules monthly circus from April through October, weather permitting.
  • Makes sure 4 duties for monthly circus are filled every circus. 4 Duties are: Announcements, hiking, climbing, and food.
TRS Chair
  • Schedules and ensures weekly trs have a trs leader + topic.
  • organizes trs leader recruitment events 2x a year.
Adventure Race Chair
  • reviving a dead position this year
    • Organizes annual adventure race in July.
    Social Chair
    • Organize or allocates monthly movie night, party, bbq, or trip report.
    Trip Whip
    • Motivate leaders and make sure that there is 1 independent trip happening every weekend.

    Activity Chairs:

    3 Season Hiking Chair
    • Recruits 3 season hiking leaders (primarily for Circus but also independent trips)
    • Makes sure leaders are qualified to lead trips independently.
    Climbing Chair
    • Recruits climbing leaders (primarily for Circus and WS but also independent trips)
    • Makes sure leaders are qualified to lead trips independently and guide co-leaders on skills to become a leader.
    • Organizes or allocates self rescue course in the spring.
    • Organizes Learn-to-climb series.
    • Maintains climbing and ice climbing gear.
    Mountaineering Chair
    • Organizes or allocates duty of organizing Avy course and Glacier Travel course.
    • Organizes or allocates duty of organizing mountaineering portion of Learn-to-climb series.
    • Maintains restricted mountaineering gear.
    BC ski Chair
    • Recruits ski leaders (primarily for WS)
    • Maintains ski gear.
    Sea Kayak Chair
    • Maintains sea kayak boats
    • Holds sea kayaking trips
    Whitewater Chair
    • Maintains whitewater boats
    • Holds whitewater trips
    Canoe Chair
    • Maintains canoe boats
    • Holds canoe trips
    Windsurfing Chair
    • Maintains windsurfing gear
    • Holds windsurfing trips
    Mountain Biking Chair
    • Holds mountain biking trips

    Managers:

    Desk Manager
    • Schedules shift desk workers for every office hour.
    • Train new desk workers and ensure there are 5-6 shift desk workers available to work desk each season.
    • Makes sure renters with gear over 1 month overdue are reminded of gear policy 3x and that if gear is not returned after 10 weeks, to cash the deposit check.
    Gear Manager
    • Make sure broken gear is fixed/thrown out/sold and replaced.
    • Make sure all rental gear is suitable to rent out.
    • Manage pro deals.
    • Add new or missing gear to database.
    Climbing Wall Manager
    • Reset wall once every 3 months and train new staff and setters.
    • Organize annual climbing competition in May.
    Camelot Manager
    • Manage cabin rentals.
    • Maintain general upkeep of cabin and other cabin projects.
    • Hold keyholder trainings 2-4x a year.
    Intervale Manager
    • Manage cabin rentals.
    • Maintain general upkeep of cabin and other cabin projects.
    • Hold keyholder trainings 2-4x a year.
    Boathouse Manager
    • Hold keyholder trainings in summer months once a month.
    Webmaster
    • Regularly update website.
    • Maintain database.
    Archivist/Historian
    • Maintain and digitize MITOC archives.
