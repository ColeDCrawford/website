3 SEASON HIKING LEADERS

What constitutes a "3-season hike"?

We consider any hike "3-season" if winter-conditions are not present. Nominally ~April-October but this varies vastly year to year. During shoulder seasons it is up to the winter safety committee to decide whether conditions are 3 season or winter.

Why does MITOC need 3-season hiking leaders?

MITOC runs all kinds of official hiking trips during the year outside of winter -at monthy circuses, fall fling, lake george, Acadia and many more - and all of these trips rely on safe, experienced hiking leaders. It is important to create a group of approved leaders that can lead 3-season hikes so all trips will go smoothly, safely, and enjoyably for all participants.

What are the leader benefits?

  • Free gear rental from MITOC for trips led
  • Gas subsidy at $0.13/mile ($60 max per trip, limit to 1 car, cannot be applied to trips used for course subsidy)
  • Course subsidy for your Wilderness First Aid certification (WFA, WAFA, WFR, WEMT)

Why should you become a leader?

  • Come meet fellow enthusiasts!
  • Help others enjoy the outdoors!
  • Perform for your group on the summit!

How do I become a leader?

Please fill out the form below. This is an informal "resume" summarzing both 3 season hiking and outdoor leadership experience. We also require attend a ~30 minute safety seminar given throughout the year. Applicants will be assigned a "Leader" or "Co-leader" rating based on the resume. Co-leaders will need to co-lead a trip with a full leader, but can be considered for full-leader status after they have co-lead a trip or a few.

Can I lead a hike outside of the MITOC circuses?

Yes! If you are an approved leader you just need to notify the hiking chair that you want to run an official trip and announce it to mitoc@mit.edu.
