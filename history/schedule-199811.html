Sun, 9/27		 West River Whitewater Weekend Bill Finger
fingew@mit.edu		 The festival is a gathering of paddlers from all across New England, and sometimes there are good deals on used equipment. Members from several outing clubs, notably us and RPI, will be in attendance. The river itself has two sections. The bottom section is a pleasant class II, although there some long swims if you miss a roll. The upper section is class III, with one fairly technical section. We will leave Friday evening, and then camp like true outdoors folk in a campground near the river. Breakfast and lunch will be up to individuals, but the local church runs an all you can eat pasta bash that shouldn't be missed.

Thurs, 10/1 Moonlight Canoeing on the Charles Bonnie Friedman
bonnie@math.mit.edu
617/253-2685		 Hostelling International - American Youth Hostel (HI-AYH) Trip. What could be more blissful than paddling a canoe down the Charles under a full moon? Hearken back to the heyday of canoeing at the turn of the century. This is a revival of one of the most popular activities of that time. We will have a quick canoe instruction for novices before paddling off into setting sun and an evening of relaxation and conversation. The moon will rise to light our way. Organized potluck in the canoes on the river. Bring $12 (per person for canoe rental), and an appetizer, main dish, dessert or beverage to share. Rain dates will be arranged if necessary.

Mon 10/12		 MITOC Annual Fall Circus Luke Sosnowski
lukesos@mit.edu		 Each year the Outing Club organizes a fall circus on Columbus Day weekend. The group travels out the the MIT cabin at Camelot, where people participate in a variety of outdoors activities, including canoeing, hiking, and rock climbing. There are always hikes and climbs for outdoorsmen/women or all skill levels. Beginners are especially encouraged to use fall circus as an introduction to the Outing Club and the activities that it offers.

Sun, 11/1		 Presidentials Hike Tarjei Mikkelsen
tarjei@mit.edu		 Two day hike in the Presidential range. We'll start out from Pinkham Notch, climb Mt. Madison and set up camp in the Great Gulf. The next day we'll ascend Mt. Washington and return via Boott Spur or Tuckerman. Both days will include 10+ miles of hiking and an altitude gain of 3-4000 feet. Some parts of the trail are a bit tricky and the weather in the White Mountains region is highly unpredictable, so this trip will not be suitable for beginners.
