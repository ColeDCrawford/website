Winter School 2004

Introduction

Every year MITOC organizes a set of lectures, demonstrations, and winter trips know collectively as Winter School. This year the tradition will continue with a variety of lectures and a multitude of trips for all skill levels.

The lectures cover such topics as proper clothing and gear, food, snow shelters, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, mountaineering, and more. Each weekend during IAP we organize multiple trips to the White Mountains to practice the skills learned in the lectures. You can rent most of the technical equipment needed for these trips from MITOC.

There is no charge for winter school and you do not need to register in advance. Just show up to the weekly lectures! However, to enhance everyone's safety and enjoyment of the mountains we do require that you attend all of the mandatory lectures in order to participate in our weekend trips. (Trips usually have small charges associated with them, to cover costs of gear rental, gas, food, land use fees, etc.)

IAP Description:

No enrollment limit, no advance sign up
Participants welcome at individual sessions (series)
Prereq: none
Introduction to self-propelled wilderness winter travel. Two nights per week of lecture, plus trips every weekend for people who have been to the classes (rental gear available). Topics progress from daytrips to winter camping and mountaineering; will cover food, clothing, and travel by snowshoes, crampons and skis. Talks will include info on winter natural history, weather, map & compass skills, self-arrest, safety, and cold weather injuries. All levels of experience welcome. NOTE: THE FIRST SEVERAL LECTURES ARE CUMULATIVE AND MANDATORY FOR PARTICIPATION IN WINTER TRIPS!

Weekend Trips

There will be trips every weekend, based on the materials built on the previous lectures. The sign ups will occur on Thursday. There will be office hours on Thrusday right after lectures to obtain the material for the weekends. Transportation and leaders are usually the limiting factors, so if you have friends with cars, encourage them to come.

If you or a friend have some winter/cold weather experience, please consider leading or co-leading a trip; contact us at mitoc-owner@mit.edu to sign up.

In order to participate in a weekend trip, you need to attend all mandatory lectures up to and including the last Thursday before the trip weekend. Individual trip leaders have the final decision if they do not feel a person has the proper knowledge or preparation for a trip.

The below schedule of trips is very tentative, and not complete. These trips could change, and there will be many other trips announced on Thursday lectures.

Do NOT email the trip leaders to sign up for the trips. Trip signup will be done in person at Winter School letures. Trip leader email addresses are provided in case you have questions about the trips.
