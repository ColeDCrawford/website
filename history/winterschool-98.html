Winter School 1998

Introduction

Every year MITOC organizes a set of lectures, demonstrations, and winter trips know collectively as Winter School. This year the tradition will continue with a variety of lectures and a multitude of trips for all skill levels.

The lectures cover such topics as proper clothing and gear, food, snow shelters, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, mountaineering, and more. Each weekend during IAP we organize multiple trips to the White Mountains to practice the skills learned in the lectures. You can rent most of the technical equipment needed for these trips from MITOC.

There is no charge for winter school and you do not need to register in advance. Just show up to the weekly lectures! However, to enhance everyone's safety and enjoyment of the mountains we do require that you attend all of the mandatory Wednesday lectures in order to participate on our weekend trips.

Trips

Wednesday lectures are mandatory. In order to participate in a weekend trip, you need to attend all mandatory lectures up to and including the last Wednesday before the trip weekend. Individual trip leaders have the final decision if they do not feel a person has the proper knowledge or preparation for a trip.

If you are interested in leading a MITOC trip, please contact mitoc-owner@mit.edu.

The below schedule of trips is very tentative, and not complete. These trips could change, and there will be many other trips announced on Wednesday lectures.

Do NOT email the trip leaders to sign up for the trips. Trip signup will be done in person at Winter School letures. Trip leader email addresses are provided in case you have questions about the trips.
