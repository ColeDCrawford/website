Introduction

If you are interested in learning about how to enjoy the outdoors in the winter, then MITOC's Winter School, offered over IAP, is for you. Winter School combines lectures (twice a week for four weeks) with weekend trips to the mountains.

The lectures cover such topics as proper clothing and gear, food, snow shelters, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, mountaineering, and more. They are designed for people who have no previous winter camping experience--beginners are especially invited. Talks are often accompanied by slides from MITOC members' expeditions around New England and around the world.

Each weekend during IAP we organize multiple trips to the White Mountains to practice the skills learned in the lectures. On the first weekend are day hikes, while on the later weekends we go on overnight trips, culminating in an attempt on Mt. Washington, the highest peak in the Presidentials.

There is no charge for winter school and you do not need to register in advance. Just show up to the weekly lectures! However, to enhance everyone's saftety and enjoyment of the mountains we do require that you attend all of the lectures in order to participate on our weekend trips.

Murray Hamlet

One of the major attractions of Winter School is the annual lecture by Dr. Murray Hamlet of the US Army on "Cold Weather Injuries." Dr. Hamlet gives an informative, entertaining, and not a little disturbing talk on how to understand and prevent cold weather injuries such as frostbite and hypothermia. His slides are truly a must-see. Watch this space, or the MITOC board in the infinite corridor, for the date and time of Dr. Hamlet's lecture this January.
